George Raymond Richard Martin

The writer of A Song of Ice and Fire epic Saga

Here's a time line of Martin's life:

  • 1948 - Born in Bayonne, New Jersey.
  • 1953 - His family moved to a near to a Bayonne docks, where he began to write monster histories for pennies.
  • 1970 - Earn the B.S. in journalism from Northwestern University's Medill School of Journalism.
  • 1971 - Sell his first short history "The Hero" to Galaxy magazine.
  • 1973 - Published "With Morning Comes Mistfall", in Analog magazine, his first story nominated for the Hugo Award and Nebula Awards.
  • 1975 - Won the Hugo Award for Best Novella for "A Song for Lya".
  • 1977 - Published his first novel "Dying of the Light".
  • 1984 - Accept Betsy Mitchell's offer and wrote some more Tuf stories which were collected in Tuf Voyaging due lacking of money.
  • 1987 - Continued working in print media as a book-series editor, this time overseeing the development of the multi-author "Wild Cards" book series.
  • 1991 - He returned to writing novels due to his production limitations related to television. This eventually turned into his epic fantasy serie: "A Song of Ice and Fire", inspired by The War of the Roses, The Accursed Kings and Ivanhoe.
  • 1996 - Published the first book: "A Game of Thrones".
  • 1998 - Published the second book: "A Clash of Kings".
  • 2000 - Published the third book: "A Storm of Swords".
  • 2005 - Published the fourth book: "A Feasts for Crows". Which became The New York Times No. 1 Bestseller.
  • 2007 - HBO Productions purchased the television rights for the A Song of Ice and Fire series.
  • 2011 - Published the fifth book: "A Dance with Dragons". Which became an international bestseller, including achieving a No. 1 spot on the New York Times Bestseller List.
  • 2011 - HBO began airing the fantasy series on their US premium cable channel on April 17. Titled Game of Thornes
  • 2012 - HBO The second season, based on the second A Song of Ice and Fire novel A Clash of Kings, began airing on HBO in the US on April 1.
  • 2016 - Martin announced that Universal Cable Productions had acquired the rights to adapt the Wild Cards novels into a television series.
